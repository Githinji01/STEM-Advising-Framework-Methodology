# Methodology

To operationalize my proposed endeavor developing a scalable, data-informed STEM Advising Framework for academic recovery and degree persistence among engineering students on probation. I will implement a structured, evidence-based advising and student support system grounded in developmental advising, intrusive intervention theory, and student success models. This methodology integrates Canvas-based modular learning, continuous progress monitoring, holistic student development systems, and data-informed advisor engagement into a replicable institutional framework.

The methodology addresses a persistent challenge in U.S. STEM education: high academic stringency leading to elevated attrition rates, gaps in student academic skill readiness, and limited structured intervention post-probation. Traditional advising models are reactive rather than structured and continuous, producing episodic engagement that fails to sustain academic recovery. Rather than treating advising, skill development, and wellness support as separate and sequential functions, this endeavor formalizes probation recovery as a unified, dynamic student success system requiring continuous, coordinated intervention.

The methodology consists of four interdependent pillars forming a closed-loop advising ecosystem: structured intervention through a Canvas-based modular program, academic recovery planning, continuous monitoring with advisor checkpoints, and holistic student development. These pillars are operationalized through a 16-module scaffolded curriculum, a four-phase advising workflow, and embedded accountability tools that transform probation advising from episodic consultation into systematic, data-informed academic recovery.

## Phase I: Structured Intervention - Canvas-Based Modular Program

The first phase establishes a guided learning experience that transforms advising into a scaffolded, sequential intervention embedded within Canvas. This phase directly addresses the lack of structured post-probation programming by providing a 16-module program that guides students through orientation, skill development, academic planning, continuous monitoring, and outcome evaluation.

### Key Method Components

This phase includes:

- Deployment of a 16-module scaffolded program embedded within Canvas with sequential progression (lock/unlock structure)
- Foundation phase modules (1–4) covering academic awareness, accountability development, and goal setting
- Skill development phase modules (5–8) addressing study strategies, time management, and academic behavior formation
- Application phase modules (9–14) implementing GPA tracking, academic adjustments, and intervention strategies
- Evaluation phase modules (15–16) conducting performance review, recovery assessment, and next-step planning
- Embedded assessments, reflections, and advisor-integrated checkpoints at critical intervention points

### Measurable Outputs

- Fully operational 16-module Canvas advising program with sequential lock/unlock progression
- Documented module completion rates across foundation, skill development, application, and evaluation phases
- Advisor-integrated checkpoint completion at midterm and final recovery assessment milestones

## Phase II: Academic Recovery Planning and Progression Framework

The second phase converts structured intervention into actionable academic recovery through GPA strategy modeling, course scheduling logic, and progress tracking systems. This framework shifts academic planning from reactive course selection to data-informed recovery trajectory mapping aligned with institutional policy and degree completion requirements.

### Key Method Components

This phase includes:

- Term-specific GPA threshold modeling with recovery trajectory mapping aligned to institutional policy
- Course scheduling logic optimizing load balance between rigor and credit hours
- Repeat versus progression decision-making protocols with advisor validation
- Progress tracking through GPA projections, midterm grade evaluation, and term-end academic audits
- Strategic course planning modules including GPA target setting with projection logic

### Measurable Outputs

- Documented GPA recovery trajectories with term-specific threshold benchmarks
- Advisor-validated course scheduling plans for probation students
- Term-end academic audit reports with midterm adjustment records

## Phase III: Continuous Monitoring and Accountability Systems

The third phase integrates weekly and monthly academic tracking with embedded accountability tools to ensure sustained student engagement throughout the recovery process. Continuous monitoring enables data-informed advising sessions and timely midterm adjustments before academic standing decisions become irreversible.

### Key Method Components

This phase includes:

- Weekly monitoring of study time reporting, attendance, assignment tracking, and behavioral reflection surveys
- Monthly monitoring through academic planner submissions and progress trend analysis
- Embedded accountability tools including Canvas quizzes for comprehension and self-check GPA awareness tools
- Data-informed advising sessions triggered by monitoring checkpoints
- Midterm intervention adjustments based on real-time academic performance data

### Measurable Outputs

- Weekly and monthly monitoring completion rates across enrolled probation students
- Documented midterm intervention adjustments and advisor checkpoint records
- Progress trend analysis reports supporting data-informed advising decisions

## Phase IV: Holistic Student Development and Resource Alignment

The fourth phase ensures that academic recovery is supported by integrated wellness, balance, and institutional resource systems. Holistic development addresses the multidimensional factors—academic, emotional, physical, social, financial, career, and environmental—that influence long-term STEM student persistence beyond GPA improvement alone.

### Key Method Components

This phase includes:

- Integration of seven dimensions of wellness across academic, emotional, physical, social, financial, career, and environmental pathways
- Balance framework implementation covering school–work–life prioritization, time-blocking strategies, and burnout mitigation techniques
- Resource alignment connecting students to tutoring, supplemental instruction, faculty engagement, and peer support systems
- Advisor role expansion as coach, data interpreter, strategic planner, and connector to institutional resources

### Measurable Outputs

- Documented resource utilization frequency across tutoring, faculty engagement, and support services
- Student wellness pathway engagement metrics across seven dimensions
- Advisor connection records linking students to appropriate institutional support systems

## Phase V: Advising Workflow Integration and Operational Deployment

The fifth phase operationalizes the four-pillar ecosystem through a structured four-phase advising workflow spanning intake, structured engagement, monitoring and intervention, and outcome evaluation. This workflow ensures that the methodology integrates with existing institutional probation processes rather than operating as a standalone initiative.

### Key Method Components

This phase includes:

- Phase 1 intake procedures for probation identification and program enrollment
- Phase 2 structured engagement requiring module completion and weekly submissions
- Phase 3 monitoring and intervention with data-informed advising sessions and midterm adjustments
- Phase 4 outcome evaluation for academic standing decisions and next-term strategy planning
- System flow integration from probation status input through intervention, planning, monitoring, and wellness support to academic recovery output

### Measurable Outputs

- End-to-end advising workflow completion rates across intake through outcome evaluation
- Academic standing decision outcomes with documented next-term strategy plans
- Operational deployment records across online, hybrid, and in-person delivery environments

## Phase VI: Assessment, Scalability, and Continuous Improvement

The final phase ensures sustained impact and measurable system-wide improvement through outcome assessment, scalability validation, and adaptive framework evolution. This continuous improvement approach transforms the initiative from a single-program deployment into a transferable institutional model capable of expansion across departments, student populations, and colleges.

### Key Method Components

This phase includes:

- Performance evaluation tracking module completion rate, term GPA improvement, and resource utilization frequency
- Scalability validation across departments and multiple student populations
- Transferability assessment for online, hybrid, and in-person environments
- Future integration of predictive analytics and longitudinal student outcome tracking
- Framework expansion planning across colleges and institutions

### Measurable Outputs

- Observed outcomes including increased academic task completion, improved GPA trajectories, and higher institutional engagement
- Scalability and transferability assessment reports documenting cross-department deployment
- Longitudinal tracking framework for continuous framework refinement and national expansion

## Distinction from Routine Employment Duties

This methodology is not limited to performing standard academic advising tasks for a single institution. Rather, it synthesizes intrusive advising theory, self-regulated learning theory, student persistence models, Canvas-based modular curriculum design, continuous monitoring systems, and holistic wellness integration into a unified, scalable advising framework. The structured integration of these components demonstrates a national STEM student success initiative rather than routine probation counseling or course registration support.

Conventional advising approaches treat academic planning, skill development, and wellness support as separate and episodic functions, resulting in delayed interventions and fragmented student engagement. This endeavor formalizes probation recovery as a unified, dynamic student success system in which proactive advisor engagement, structured learning modules, and continuous data-informed monitoring operate as coordinated components—a fundamentally different scope than day-to-day academic advising appointments.

## Anticipated National Impact

Implementation of this methodology is expected to:

- Improve STEM student persistence and degree completion rates through structured academic recovery pathways
- Reduce attrition among engineering students on probation by replacing episodic advising with continuous, data-informed intervention
- Strengthen student accountability, metacognition, and self-regulated learning behaviors essential for long-term academic success
- Increase institutional engagement with tutoring, faculty support, and wellness resources among at-risk student populations
- Enable scalable advising delivery through learning management systems accessible to online, hybrid, and in-person environments
- Provide a transferable framework adaptable across departments, colleges, and institutions serving diverse STEM student populations

Observed implementation outcomes confirm consistent improvements across key performance dimensions: increased academic task completion, improved GPA trajectories, and higher institutional engagement. Future integration of predictive analytics and longitudinal outcome tracking will further strengthen the framework's capacity to support national STEM education priorities and workforce development goals.
